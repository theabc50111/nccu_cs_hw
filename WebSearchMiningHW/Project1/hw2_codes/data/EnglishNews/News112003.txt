Breakingviews - Review: “Empireland” puts modern Britain on couch
LONDON (Reuters Breakingviews) - Before he was pitched from his plinth into Bristol’s harbour by protestors last summer, few in Britain had heard of Edward Colston. Yet the unceremonious dunking of the 17th century slave-trader’s statue forced a rare moment of self-questioning about the empire on which, as every Briton knows, “the sun never set”. As Sathnam Sanghera argues in his timely “Empireland: How Imperialism Has Shaped Modern Britain”, the collective amnesia over the nation’s often-dark colonial past underpins the jingoism that helped spawned policy disasters from Suez to Brexit. With luck, the book can be part of the solution.
