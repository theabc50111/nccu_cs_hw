Breakingviews - Danone’s governance fudge sours activist defence
LONDON (Reuters Breakingviews) - Danone’s governance shakeup falls short. The 39 billion euro French yoghurt maker on Monday said it would split the roles of chief executive and chairman, after pressure from activist shareholders. But incumbent Emmanuel Faber will stay on for now in the latter role. It’s hardly a move that suggests an appetite for change.
