Breakingviews - Cox: Trump Fed misfire rallies its true believers
New York (Reuters Breakingviews) - In June 1971 Arthur Burns, then chairman of the Federal Reserve, sent U.S. President Richard Nixon a nine-page letter in which he summed up the country’s economic challenges: “namely, an inflation feeding on itself at a time of substantial unemployment.” Burns told the Republican president, who had nominated him to the Fed and for whom he previous served as an adviser, that “our monetary and fiscal policies have not been working as expected.”
